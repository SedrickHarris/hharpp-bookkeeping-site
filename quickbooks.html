QuickBooks Desktop 2023 sunsets May 31, 2026 - migration help available now

On May 31, 2026, QuickBooks Desktop 2023 loses payroll tax tables, bank feed connections, and all security patches. If you're still on Desktop 2023, you have a hard deadline. Hharpp migrates your company file to QuickBooks Online, validates every report against your Desktop data, and ensures your payroll and integrations are running correctly before you close the old file.

1
Pre-migration file cleanup
Reconcile all accounts, merge duplicates, and clear Undeposited Funds before export. A clean Desktop file migrates cleanly. A messy one migrates messy.
2
Company file migration
Hharpp runs Intuit's migration tool, transfers chart of accounts, customer and vendor lists, and up to 24 months of transaction history - then validates the trial balance against Desktop before you proceed.
3
Payroll history migration
Employee YTD balances, deduction configurations, and tax setup do not automatically transfer. Hharpp rebuilds this manually in QBO Payroll so your first post-migration payroll run is correct.
4
Post-migration validation
Compare trial balance, A/R aging, A/P aging, and bank balances between Desktop and QBO. Discrepancies caught now are fixed in hours. Discrepancies caught at tax time cost thousands.

Contractors have different QuickBooks needs than retail or service businesses. Job costing, progress invoicing, certified payroll, subcontractor 1099 tracking, and Buildertrend integration all require a setup that understands construction accounting - not just accounting software.

Contractor chart of accounts
Labor, materials, subcontractors, equipment, overhead - structured correctly by cost type and mapped to job categories. Not the default template. Built for a contractor P&L that actually shows job profitability.
Job costing setup (Classes + Projects)
QuickBooks Plus and Advanced support job costing via Projects. Heather configures class tracking and project setup so your cost reports show profitability by job, not just by account. This is the setup that prevents the 'we thought we were making money' problem.
Buildertrend + QuickBooks integration
