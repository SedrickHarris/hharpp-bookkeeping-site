What determines the cost of your Hharpp payroll quote

No two businesses have the same payroll. The five factors below are what Hharpp reviews in every free consultation to build a scope that fits your situation - not a billing structure that charges you for complexity you don't have.

Employee count
More employees means more pay runs, more tax calculations, more W-2s at year-end, and more compliance surface area. It's the single largest driver of payroll service cost in any market.
1–5 employees versus 20–50 employees represents very different scope
Pay frequency
Weekly payroll requires 52 processing cycles per year. Monthly is 12. Biweekly is 26. More pay periods means more processing time, more tax deposits, and more reconciliation against your books.
Weekly payroll is roughly twice the processing work of monthly
Compliance complexity
Nevada-only employers have simpler obligations than businesses operating in both Nevada and California. Construction companies with certified payroll and prevailing wage reporting under NRS 338 carry significantly higher compliance load - and that scope is priced accordingly.
Certified payroll under NRS 338.070 adds monthly reporting requirements
Worker mix
Businesses with a mix of W-2 employees and 1099 independent contractors require classification review, separate filing workflows, and 1099-NEC preparation at year-end - all of which affect scope.
All-W-2 versus mixed W-2 and 1099 affects year-end scope materially
Bundled versus standalone
Payroll bundled with Hharpp's bookkeeping service means a single engagement for both - eliminating redundant setup and coordination. Clients who bundle payroll and bookkeeping typically see better value than engaging both services separately.
Bookkeeping and payroll in the same QuickBooks file from the same person
Initial setup versus ongoing
New employers requiring DETR registration, MBT configuration, QuickBooks Payroll setup, and first-run review carry a defined one-time setup scope on top of ongoing monthly processing. Established employers with a running system have a narrower initial scope.
First-employee setup is a defined scope; ongoing runs are a recurring scope
